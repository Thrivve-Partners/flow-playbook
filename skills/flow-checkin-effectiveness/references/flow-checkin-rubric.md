# Flow Check-in Effectiveness Rubric

Detailed scoring criteria for evaluating standup transcripts. All criteria reference standards from `Flow Checkin.md`.

---

## 1. Invisible Work Check (1 point)

**Reference:** Flow Checkin.md - "Before We Look at the Board"

### What to Look For

- Did anyone ask: "Is there anything you're working on that isn't on the board?"
- Was the 30-minute rule mentioned or enforced? ("If it's taking 30 minutes or more of your time, it needs a ticket")
- Did anyone mention work not on the board and create a ticket for it?

### Scoring

- **1.0** - Invisible work explicitly checked, 30-minute rule applied
- **0.5** - Mentioned but not systematically checked
- **0.0** - Not addressed

### Evidence to Capture

Direct quotes showing invisible work discussion (or absence)

---

## 2. Question 1: What's Stuck or Ageing? (3 points)

**Reference:** Flow Checkin.md - "The Three Questions - 1. What's stuck or ageing?"

### 2a. Ageing Items Identified (1 point)

#### What to Look For

- Are they looking at items by age (oldest first)?
- Are items flagged when approaching 50% of SLE?
- Are items swarmed when at 70% of SLE?
- Do they reference Work Item Age charts or ageing metrics?

#### Scoring

- **1.0** - Age-based walk, items flagged at thresholds, swarming offered
- **0.5** - Age mentioned but not systematically walked
- **0.0** - No age focus, column-based walk only

#### Evidence to Capture

Quotes showing age discussion (or absence)

### 2b. Blocker Management (1 point)

#### What to Look For

- Are blockers specifically named (not just "it's blocked")?
- Is someone assigned to own escalation?
- Are blockers properly categorised (Level 1/2/3)?
  - **Level 1:** Blocked, actively seeking resolution, counts against WIP, owned escalation
  - **Level 2:** Resolution expected, escalation window passed, stays on board ageing
  - **Level 3:** No stateable resolution, split/cancel/clone

#### Scoring

- **1.0** - Blockers named, owned, escalation clear, levels applied
- **0.5** - Blockers mentioned but not owned or categorised
- **0.0** - Blockers not addressed or vague ("waiting on X")

#### Evidence to Capture

- Blocker naming and ownership examples
- Categorisation references (or absence)

### 2c. Swarming on Ageing Work (1 point)

#### What to Look For

- When work isn't moving, does the team offer to swarm?
- When someone says "still where I was yesterday", is help offered?
- Are non-moving items treated as flow problems requiring intervention?

#### Scoring

- **1.0** - Swarming actively offered when work stalls
- **0.5** - Swarming recognised but not actively coached
- **0.0** - Non-moving work treated as normal status update

#### Evidence to Capture

- Swarming offers (or missed opportunities)
- Responses to static work

---

## 3. Question 2: WIP Limits & Policies (2 points)

**Reference:** Flow Checkin.md - "The Three Questions - 2. Are we within our WIP limits, and using our Policies effectively?"

### 3a. WIP Limit Check (1 point)

#### What to Look For

- Did they check: "Are we within our WIP limits?"
- If over: "What needs to finish before anything new starts?"
- If slack: Did they look right on the board before pulling new work?
- "Work as a team on what's already in flight before anyone considers pulling anything new"

#### Scoring

- **1.0** - WIP limits explicitly checked, over/under addressed appropriately
- **0.5** - WIP mentioned but not systematically checked
- **0.0** - WIP limits not referenced

#### Evidence to Capture

WIP limit discussions (or absence)

### 3b. Pull Policy & Criteria (1 point)

#### What to Look For

- Is "finish before pull" applied or mentioned?
- Are pull criteria referenced? ("Work moves because it meets agreed criteria")
- When someone looks for work, is there a check: "Can we finish existing work first?"
- Is the expedite policy mentioned? (one item at a time, swarmed until done)

#### Scoring

- **1.0** - Pull policy explicitly applied, criteria referenced
- **0.5** - Pull policy recognised but not consistently applied
- **0.0** - No pull policy evident, work pulled without criteria check

#### Evidence to Capture

- Finish-before-pull checks (or missed opportunities)
- Pull criteria applications

---

## 4. Question 3: What's Moving Today? (1 point)

**Reference:** Flow Checkin.md - "The Three Questions - 3. What's moving today?"

### What to Look For

- Is the team clear on what progresses today?
- Do people leave knowing exactly what they're working on?
- If nothing ready to pull, is backlog readiness discussed?

### Scoring

- **1.0** - Clear commitments on what moves today, backlog readiness addressed if needed
- **0.5** - Some clarity but vague commitments
- **0.0** - No clear next steps, team leaves unclear

### Evidence to Capture

Statements about what's moving today (or absence)

---

## 5. Pattern Recognition (1 point)

**Reference:** Flow Checkin.md - "When the Conversation Goes Deeper"

### What to Look For

- Are recurring patterns noted for flow review (not solved in standup)?
- Common triggers:
  - Multiple items ageing simultaneously across the same stage
  - Same blocker types surfacing repeatedly
  - WIP consistently at or over limit
  - Backlog regularly empty
- "Note them, name them, and move on. Solving them in the daily check-in is the wrong conversation in the wrong room."

### Scoring

- **1.0** - Patterns noted, deferred to flow review appropriately
- **0.5** - Patterns emerging but not explicitly noted
- **0.0** - Long discussions solving systemic issues in standup (wrong forum)

### Evidence to Capture

Pattern discussions (or long problem-solving in standup)

---

## 6. Overall Health (2 points)

**Reference:** Flow Checkin.md - "What Good Looks Like"

### 6a. Routine Feel & Honest Board (1 point)

#### What to Look For

- Does the check-in feel routine (not chaotic)?
- Is work moving?
- Are blockers named early and owned?
- Is the board an honest picture of where capacity is going?
- "If the check-in regularly surfaces surprises, the system needs attention, not a longer meeting."

#### Scoring

- **1.0** - Routine feel, no surprises, honest board, work moving
- **0.5** - Some surprises or chaos, but generally functional
- **0.0** - Chaotic, many surprises, unclear state

#### Evidence to Capture

Overall tone and clarity (or confusion)

### 6b. Clear Next Steps (1 point)

#### What to Look For

- "The team leaves knowing exactly what the day looks like"
- Are commitments clear?
- Do people know what they're working on?

#### Scoring

- **1.0** - Team leaves with clear next steps
- **0.5** - Some clarity but vague in places
- **0.0** - Unclear what people are doing next

#### Evidence to Capture

Closing statements and clarity of commitments

## Interpretation Guide

- **8-10:** Excellent - Flow Check-in standards well embedded, minor refinements only
- **6-7:** Good - Core practices present, needs consistent application
- **4-5:** Developing - Some practices emerging, significant coaching needed
- **2-3:** Early - Few practices present, intensive coaching required
- **0-1:** Not Present - Flow Check-in standards not yet introduced

---

## Common Patterns by Score Range

### Early (2-3/10)
- Column-based walk (not age-based)
- Blockers mentioned but not owned
- Swarming happening organically but not coached when work stalls
- No WIP limit checks
- Pull policy emerging but not explicit
- Long technical discussions in standup

### Developing (4-5/10)
- Some age awareness but not systematic walk
- Blockers named and owned but not categorised
- Swarming requested in some cases
- Finish-before-pull thinking evident but implicit
- Patterns emerging but not explicitly noted
- Some foundational practices present

### Good (6-7/10)
- Age-based walk happening most of the time
- Blockers named, owned, and beginning to be categorised
- Swarming actively offered when work stalls
- WIP limits checked regularly
- Pull policy explicit in most cases
- Patterns noted and deferred appropriately

### Excellent (8-10/10)
- Systematic age-based walk every standup
- Blockers explicitly categorised with escalation protocol
- Swarming immediate when work doesn't move
- WIP limits checked and acted upon
- Pull policy consistently applied with criteria
- Pattern recognition automatic
- Routine feel, no surprises, clear next steps
