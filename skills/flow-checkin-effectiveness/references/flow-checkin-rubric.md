# Flow Check-in Effectiveness Rubric

Detailed scoring criteria for evaluating standup transcripts. All criteria reference standards from `practices/flow-check-in.md`.

---

## 1. Invisible Work Check (1 point)

**Reference:** flow-check-in.md - "Before We Look at the Board"

### What to Look For

- Did anyone ask: "Is there anything you're working on that isn't on the board?"
- Was the 30-minute rule mentioned or enforced? ("If it's taking 30 minutes or more of your time, it needs a ticket")
- Did anyone mention work not on the board and create a ticket for it?

### Scoring

- **1.0** — Invisible work explicitly checked, 30-minute rule applied
- **0.5** — Mentioned but not systematically checked
- **0.0** — Not addressed

### Evidence to Capture

Direct quotes showing invisible work discussion (or absence)

---

## 2. Question 1: What's Stuck or Ageing? (3 points)

**Reference:** flow-check-in.md - "The Three Questions - 1. What's stuck or ageing?"

### 2a. Ageing Items Identified (1 point)

#### What to Look For

- Are they looking at items by age (oldest first)?
- Are items flagged when approaching 50% of SLE?
- Are items swarmed when at 70% of SLE?
- Do they reference the Work Item Age chart or ageing metrics?

#### Scoring

- **1.0** — Age-based walk, items flagged at SLE thresholds, oldest-first structure
- **0.5** — Age mentioned but not systematically walked; oldest item named without SLE anchor
- **0.0** — No age focus; column-based walk only

#### Evidence to Capture

Quotes showing age discussion (or absence); note whether SLE thresholds are named

### 2b. Blocker Management (1 point)

#### What to Look For

- Are blockers specifically named (not just "it's blocked")?
- Is someone assigned to own escalation?
- Are blockers properly categorised (Level 1/2/3)?
  - **Level 1:** Blocked, actively seeking resolution, counts against WIP, owned escalation
  - **Level 2:** Resolution expected, escalation window passed, stays on board ageing
  - **Level 3:** No stateable resolution — split, cancel, or clone

#### Scoring

- **1.0** — Blockers named, owned, escalation clear, Level 1/2/3 framework applied
- **0.5** — Blockers mentioned but not owned or categorised
- **0.0** — Blockers not addressed or vague ("we're waiting on X")

#### Evidence to Capture

- Blocker naming and ownership examples
- Categorisation references (or absence)

### 2c. Swarming on Ageing Work (1 point)

#### What to Look For

- When work isn't moving, does the team offer to swarm?
- When someone says "still where I was yesterday", is help offered?
- Are non-moving items treated as flow problems requiring intervention?

#### Scoring

- **1.0** — Swarming actively offered when work stalls; team redirects to help
- **0.5** — Swarming happening organically but not coached or facilitated
- **0.0** — Non-moving work treated as normal status update; no offer of help

#### Evidence to Capture

- Swarming offers or redirections (or missed opportunities)
- Responses to static work

---

## 3. Question 2: WIP Limits & Policies (2 points)

**Reference:** flow-check-in.md - "The Three Questions - 2. Are we within our WIP limits, and using our Policies effectively?"

### 3a. WIP Limit Check (1 point)

#### What to Look For

- Did they check: "Are we within our WIP limits?"
- If over: "What needs to finish before anything new starts?"
- If under/slack: Did they look right on the board before pulling new work?
- "Work as a team on what's already in flight before anyone considers pulling anything new"

#### Scoring

- **1.0** — WIP limits explicitly checked across all columns; over/under addressed appropriately
- **0.5** — WIP mentioned or noted for one column but not checked systematically
- **0.0** — WIP limits not referenced

#### Evidence to Capture

WIP limit discussions (or absence); note whether check covers all columns or just one

### 3b. Pull Policy & Criteria (1 point)

#### What to Look For

- Is "finish before pull" applied or mentioned?
- Are pull criteria referenced? ("Work moves because it meets agreed criteria")
- When someone looks for work, is there a check: "Can we finish existing work first?"
- Is pull policy mentioned in relation to expedites or new work?

#### Scoring

- **1.0** — Pull policy explicitly applied; criteria referenced before new work is pulled
- **0.5** — Finish-before-pull instinct present but not formalised; implicit sequencing
- **0.0** — No pull policy evident; work pulled without criteria check

#### Evidence to Capture

- Finish-before-pull checks (or missed opportunities)
- Pull criteria applications or informal sequencing

---

## 4. Question 3: What's Moving Today? (1 point)

**Reference:** flow-check-in.md - "The Three Questions - 3. What's moving today?"

### What to Look For

- Is the team clear on what progresses today?
- Do people leave knowing exactly what they're working on?
- Are there named, individual commitments at close?
- If nothing ready to pull, is backlog readiness discussed?

### Scoring

- **1.0** — Clear commitments on what moves today; team leaves with a shared picture of the day
- **0.5** — Some clarity but vague commitments; partial picture
- **0.0** — No clear next steps; team leaves unclear on what day looks like

### Evidence to Capture

Statements about what's moving today; closing structure (or absence of it)

---

## 5. Pattern Recognition (1 point)

**Reference:** flow-check-in.md - "When the Conversation Goes Deeper"

### What to Look For

- Are recurring patterns noted for flow review (not solved in standup)?
- Common triggers:
  - Multiple items ageing simultaneously across the same stage
  - Same blocker types surfacing repeatedly
  - WIP consistently at or over limit
  - Backlog regularly empty
  - Priority signals consistently overriding flow signals
- "Note them, name them, and move on. Solving them in the daily check-in is the wrong conversation in the wrong room."

### Scoring

- **1.0** — Patterns noted and explicitly deferred to flow review
- **0.5** — Patterns emerging but not explicitly named or deferred
- **0.0** — Long discussions solving systemic issues in standup (wrong forum) or patterns present but invisible

### Evidence to Capture

Pattern discussions or long problem-solving in standup (both are useful signals)

---

## 6. Overall Health (2 points)

**Reference:** flow-check-in.md - "What Good Looks Like"

### 6a. Routine Feel & Honest Board (1 point)

#### What to Look For

- Does the check-in feel routine (not chaotic or uncertain)?
- Is work moving?
- Are blockers named early and owned?
- Is the board an honest picture of where capacity is going?
- "If the check-in regularly surfaces surprises, the system needs attention, not a longer meeting."

#### Scoring

- **1.0** — Routine feel; no surprises; honest board; work moving
- **0.5** — Some surprises or drift, but generally functional; board mostly honest
- **0.0** — Chaotic; many surprises; unclear board state; significant social drift

#### Evidence to Capture

Overall tone and clarity (or confusion); note social drift, unexpected discoveries, or uncertainty about board state

### 6b. Clear Next Steps (1 point)

#### What to Look For

- "The team leaves knowing exactly what the day looks like"
- Are per-person commitments named at close?
- Is there a structured close or does the session trail off?

#### Scoring

- **1.0** — Team leaves with clear, named next steps per person; structured close
- **0.5** — Some clarity; vague in places; close trails off rather than structured finish
- **0.0** — Unclear what people are doing next; no close structure

#### Evidence to Capture

Closing statements and clarity of individual commitments

## Interpretation Guide

- **8-10:** Excellent — Flow Check-in standards well embedded, minor refinements only
- **6-7:** Good — Core practices present, needs consistent application
- **4-5:** Developing — Some practices emerging, significant coaching needed
- **2-3:** Early — Few practices present, intensive coaching required
- **0-1:** Not Present — Flow Check-in standards not yet introduced

---

## Common Patterns by Score Range

### Not Present / Early (0-3/10)
- Column-based walk (not age-based)
- Blockers mentioned but not owned or classified
- No WIP limit checks
- Pull decisions informal or absent
- Long social content; limited flow focus
- Team leaves without clear next steps

### Developing (4-5/10)
- Some age awareness but not a systematic oldest-first walk
- Blockers named and partially owned; Level framework not yet applied
- Swarming happening organically but not facilitated
- Finish-before-pull thinking present but implicit
- Patterns emerging but not explicitly named or deferred
- Some foundational practices present; others inconsistent

### Good (6-7/10)
- Age-based walk happening most sessions
- Blockers named, owned, and beginning to be classified
- Swarming actively offered when work stalls
- WIP limits checked regularly
- Pull policy explicit in most cases
- Patterns noted and deferred appropriately

### Excellent (8-10/10)
- Systematic age-based walk every session; SLE thresholds named
- Blockers explicitly classified with Level 1/2/3 and escalation owner
- Swarming immediate when work doesn't move
- WIP limits checked across all columns and acted upon
- Pull policy consistently applied with criteria
- Pattern recognition automatic; deferred to right forum
- Routine feel; no surprises; clear per-person commitments at close

## Facilitator-Dependency Diagnostic

A useful cross-session observation: if practices appear when the regular facilitator is present but disappear when they are absent, the practices are **facilitator-dependent**, not **team-owned**. This is a significant coaching risk — if the facilitator leaves or is unavailable, the practices leave with them.

Signs of facilitator-dependency:
- Score drops significantly (>2 points) when a different person facilitates
- Practices (e.g. invisible work check, age walk) are named by the facilitator but not adopted by other team members
- Team doesn't self-organise a check-in when the facilitator is absent

The coaching goal is for all core practices to be team-level norms — visible on the board, expected every session, reproducible by anyone in the room.
